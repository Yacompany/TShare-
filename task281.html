LITTI CHOKHA

If there’s one combination that is the hallmark of Bihari cuisine, it is the Litti Chokha. This is basically a very traditional and rustic dish of spiced sattu (roasted black chickpea flour) stuffed whole wheat dough balls called Litti.

Method Of Cocking

1. In a mortar-pestle, take ½ teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera) and ½ teaspoon fennel seeds (saunf). Lightly crush and keep aside.
2. Take 1 cup roasted gram flour or roasted black chickpea flour (sattu) in a mixing bowl or pan.
3. Now, add ½ teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain), ½ teaspoon nigella seeds (kalonji) and ½ teaspoon red chili powder.
4. Next, add 1 to 2 teaspoons chopped green chilies, 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger and 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic.
5. Add 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves, ¼ teaspoon black salt and regular salt as per taste.
6. Add 2 teaspoons lemon juice.
7. Next, add 2 teaspoons mustard oil.
8. Mix very well.
9. Sprinkle 1 to 2 tablespoons water all over and mix the flour with water evenly. Add more water, if required.
