SABUDANA KHICHDI

Sabudana was introduced for the very first time during the 1860s, by Ayilyam Thirunal Rama Varma who was the ruler of the kingdom of Travancore, Kerala back then. This foreign root was used at that time to save people from a deadly famine that plagued the kingdom.

INGREDIENT'S

8. 1/2 teaspoon sendha namak (powdered rock salt).
9. 2 tablespoons coriander leaves finely chopped.
10. 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional).
11. 1 tablespoons lemon juice.

Method Of Cocking

1. Rinse the sabudana under water until the water runs clear to remove all the starch.
2. Soak the sabudana pearls for 30 minutes, then drain the water completely and sprinkle with about 2 teaspoons of water.
3. Cover and refrigerate the sabudana overnight or for a minimum of 8 hours.
4. Roast the peanuts in a non-stick pan over medium heat for 4â€“5 minutes, or until they are crunchy and the skin is brown or charred.
5. Remove the peanuts from heat and let them cool, then pulse them in a mixer jar once or twice to make a coarse powder.
6. If you're making sabudana khichdi for fasting, use sendha namak (rock salt) instead of regular salt.
7. You can add spices like turmeric and coriander, or onions, if you're not fasting.
8. Adjust the amount of green chili to taste based on your desired spice level.
