POOTHAREKULU

Dry Fruit Pootharekulu/Poothareku is a traditional sweet that is originally from Atreyapuram a village in Andhra Pradesh. It has simple preparation steps of stuffing sugar, nuts and ghee in a rice starch sheet which is also known as the Pootharekulu sheet. The word ‘Pootha’ in English means coated and the word ‘Rekulu’ means sheets.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. Raw rice.
2. Powdered sugar or grated jaggery.
3. Ghee.
4. Cardamom powder.
5. Water.
6. Oil.

Item Quantity

1. 2 cups.
2. ½ cup.
3. ¼ cup.
4. 1 teaspoon.

Equipment Name Used In Dish

Method Of Cocking

1. Soak two cups of raw rice in water for around six hours. Later drain the water and grind them. Keep adding water little by little as it grinds. Grind it into a very thin batter and add more water if required.
2. Meanwhile, dry roast the nuts until they turn light brown and grind them into a coarse powder. Mix one teaspoon of cardamom powder to this and keep it aside.
3. Once the batter is ready, heat a flat-surfaced Tawa. Wipe it with a few drops of oil and let it heat.
4. Transfer the prepared batter to plate one ladle at a time.
5. You can dilute it more by adding water if needed before pouring it to the plate.
6. Let it fill the plate evenly. Take a slightly wet piece of cloth that is long enough to fit the tawa.
7. Spread and dip the cloth in the plate and cover it with the batter.
8. Now spread and pull this cloth on the hot tawa, it will leave a thin trace of batter on it and let it cook.
9. If it is not thin and transparent enough mix more water into the batter.
10. Once it is cooked well the sheet will lift up. Remove and place it aside.
