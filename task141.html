SOYA CHAAP ROLL

Soya chaap is a popular Indian street food made from chewy soya chunks that are bound together.

Method Of Cocking

1. Soak ½ cup of soya beans overnight.
2. Boil ½ cup of soya chunks for 5 minutes or until they are soft.
3. Squeeze out the water from the soya chunks and transfer them to a blender.
4. Drain the water from the soaked soya beans and transfer them to the blender.
5. Blend the soya beans and soya chunks into a smooth paste, adding water as needed.
6. Transfer the soya bean and soya chunk paste to a large mixing bowl.
7. Add 1 cup of maida and salt to taste.
8. Add some water and knead a dough.
9. Flatten the dough like a chapatti and cut long pieces out of it.
10. Roll the long pieces onto ice cream sticks.
11. Boil water in a pan and cook the soya chaap sticks for 10 minutes until they are slightly brown.
12. Remove the soya chaap from the sticks and cut into 1-inch pieces.
13. Heat about half a cup of oil in a pot or large saucepan.
14. Fry the chaap until golden brown and slightly crisp on all sides, which should take about 10 minutes.
15. Marinate the soya chaap in yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric, chaat masala, and salt.
16 Serve soya chaap curry along with rice, roti, or chapathi.
