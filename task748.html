PANASA POTTU AVAA KORA

Shredded/grated raw jackfruit is called Panasa Pottu in Telugu. The traditional process of shredding raw jackfruit is elaborate. There used to be a special knife used only for finely chopping jackfruit.

INGREDIENT'S

1. Jackfruit Raw (Kathal) , chopped finely.
2. Tamarind Paste.
3. Green Chillies , slit.
4. Oil.
5. Water.
6. Mustard seeds (Rai/ Kadugu).

Method Of Cocking

1. To begin making the Panasa Pottu Aava Koora, let us first make the Aava Paste.
2. In a small bowl, soak mustard seeds, sesame seeds, and red chili in hot water for 15 minutes.
3. After 15 minutes, drain the water and transfer the mustard seeds, sesame seeds, and red chili into a mixer-jar and grind to a smooth paste, using a little water if required.
4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
5. Let us now cut the jackfruit, to do that we have to first, grease our hands and the knife that we are going to use, with some oil.
6. This important tip ensures that the hands don't get sticky from the natural fruit latex/sap present in the fruit does not get onto your hands and makes the cutting process hassle-free.
7. Keep a bowl of water mixed with some salt, ready to put the cut raw jackfruit pieces to avoid discolouration of the fruit.
8. Now that your hands and knife are well greased, cut the the jackfruit in half, discard the skin and start making 1 inch pieces and soak them in the salted water.
