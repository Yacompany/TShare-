VEGETABLE KHICHDI

Khichdi is often eaten by babies and people who are sick because it soothes the stomach and intestines. It's also part of the Ayurvedic diet because it balances the three doshas, or energies, of Vatta, Pitta, and Kapha.

Method Of Cocking

1. Dry roast 1/3 cup of moong dal (husked split mung lentils) over low to medium heat until lightly golden
2. Rinse and soak the lentils in hot water for 30–40 minutes
3. Chop your vegetables
4. Sauté the vegetables in ghee for 3–4 minutes over medium heat
5. Add 1/3 cup of broken rice, 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala
6. Sauté for 2–3 minutes
7. Add 2 cups of water and bring to a boil
8. Cover and cook for 10–15 minutes over low heat, or until the rice is tender and most of the water is absorbed
9. Serve with a side of raita, pickle, plain curd, or a veggie salad, and garnish with coriander leaves
