PARUPPU PAYASAM

Paruppu payasam, also known as moong dal payasam, is a traditional South Indian pudding made from soft cooked lentils, coconut milk, jaggery, and cardamom. The dish is often tempered with ghee and fried nuts, raisins, and sometimes coconut.

Method Of Cocking

1. Dry roast the lentils for a couple of minutes until they turn golden and smell nutty.
2. Cashews add flavor and thicken the sauce.
3. Coconut milk makes the payasam creamy and silky.
4. Paruppu payasam is often offered to gods at temples and during festivals because of the coconut milk's auspiciousness.
5. A Kerala-style payasam similar to moong dal kheer but made only with coconut milk. It's often served at Onam Sadhya feasts and includes small pieces of coconut.
6. The dish is often tempered with ghee and garnished with nuts, raisins, and sometimes coconut. Paruppu payasam is usually served hot or cold and can be eaten with papad or vada.
