IMARTI

Imarti is a sweet Indian dish made from a batter of urad dal (black gram lentil) or vigna mungo flour that is deep-fried into a flower shape and then soaked in sugar syrup. It is also known by other names, including Amitti, Amriti, Emarti, Omritti, Jahangir, and Jhangiri.

Method Of Cocking

1. Soak urad dal and rice together in water for around 45 minutes.
2. Boil sugar and water in a pan until it reaches a single-thread consistency.
3. Add cardamom powder, saffron, and rose essence to the sugar syrup.
4. Heat olive oil in a shallow, flat jalebi kadai.
5. Blend the soaked urad dal with water until smooth.
6. Add food coloring and rice flour to the blended dal and mix for 3â€“4 minutes.
7. Let the batter rest for 15 minutes.
8. Pipe the batter into hot ghee to form imartis in a flower-like shape.
9. Deep fry the imartis until golden brown.
10. Soak the imartis in sugar syrup and serve.
