PANEER PASANDA

Paneer Pasanda recipe is made with a sweet and spicy filling, stuffed between two pieces of the popular Indian cottage cheese a.k.a paneer, then dipped, fried and served in a lusciously smooth onion-tomato gravy.

Method Of Cocking

1. On a low to medium heat, stir and simmer this mixture till the tomatoes soften.
2. When the tomatoes soften, switch off the heat. Some stock will be left in the pan; we will use it while blending or grinding the tomatoes.
3. Meanwhile, heat 2 tablespoons oil in a pan and add 1 heaping cup of chopped onions.
4. Stir and sauté the onions on a low to medium-low heat. Add a pinch of salt for the onions to brown faster.
5. Once the onions become light golden, switch off the heat. Let the onions cool.
6. Add the cooled fried onions to a high-speed blender or grinder jar. Add 4 to 5 tablespoons water.
7. Blend or grind the sautéed onions to a fine, smooth paste and set aside.
8. In the same blender or grinder, add the cooked and cooled tomato-cashew-spice mixture along with the stock from the pan.
9. Grind to a smooth and fine consistency. There should be no cashew chunks or small bit and pieces in the paste. Set this paste aside.
10. Mix everything evenly and keep aside. Check the taste and add more of the spices and salt if required.
