MEWA LASSI

Lassi is a frothy, creamy drink that originated in Punjab, India. It's made by blending fresh yogurt with water and various fruits or seasonings. The yogurt is traditionally made from water buffalo milk in Punjab, but there are many variations of lassi.

Method Of Cocking

1. Soak raisins in water for 15 minutes, then combine with curd, grated coconut, almonds, raisins, sugar, and cardamom in a grinder. Blend until thick and consistent, then pour into a glass and garnish with honey.
2. Blend 125 ml of curd, 200 ml of iced water, and chopped mango with a few dried mint leaves. Serve chilled.
3. Whisk 300 gm of plain yogurt until smooth, then add water to achieve lassi consistency. Mix in 1 ml of rose water and 10–15 rose petals, then refrigerate for at least an hour and serve chilled.
4. Combine thick chilled curd, mango cubes, sugar, and grated khoya. Top with sliced dry fruits and rose petals.
