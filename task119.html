TOM YUM (THAILAND)

Tom yum is a spicy, sour, and hot Thai soup that's usually made with shrimp. The name comes from the Thai words tom, which means "boiling," and yam, which means "mixed".

Method Of Cocking

1. Peel prawns, reserving the meat. In a pot, crush garlic, lemongrass, and chilies with a meat mallet to release flavor. Add the prawn heads and shells, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, stock, and water. Bring to a simmer, cover, and reduce heat to medium. Simmer for 10 minutes, then strain the broth and discard the prawn shells. Return the broth to the pot over low heat.
2. Add lime juice and pulp, chile peppers, lemongrass, ginger, tom yum paste, fish sauce, sugar, and chile sauce to the broth and cook for 10 minutes. Remove the chile peppers, lemongrass, and ginger slices with a slotted spoon and discard. Return cooked shrimp to the broth and remove from heat.
3. Add mushrooms and green onions to top the soup. You can also add tomato, coconut milk, soy sauce, tofu, and agave.
4. Season with salt to taste and garnish with cilantro or sawtooth coriander.
