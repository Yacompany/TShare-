MOONG DAAL CHEELA

You can make the chilla soft or crisp. For a soft chilla, pour and spread the batter lightly. For a crisp chilla, spread the batter thinly and add extra oil. You can serve moong dal chilla with pickles, chutneys, ginger chai, or curries.

Method Of Cocking

1. Rinse and soak 1 cup of moong dal in 2–3 cups of water for 3–4 hours, or until it doubles in size.
2. Discard the water and add the moong dal to a blender or grinder along with ¼ cup of water.
3. Grind or blend until smooth and fine. If the lentils aren't grinding well, you can add 1–2 tablespoons more water.
4. Add ¼ teaspoon of turmeric powder, ¼ teaspoon of red chili powder, and ½ teaspoon of roasted cumin powder or cumin powder.
5. Heat a cast iron skillet or griddle over medium heat and add oil or ghee to coat the pan.
6. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the pan and spread it into a circle from the center outwards.
7. Cook for 2–3 minutes until the underside is browned, then flip and cook until the other side is golden.
8. Serve with green chutney
