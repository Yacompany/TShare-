JHAL MURI

Jhalmuri is a popular street snack in the Bengali, Bihari and Odia cuisines of the Indian subcontinent, of Indian spices, vegetables, oil. It is popular in Bangladesh and in the neighbouring Indian states of Bihar, West Bengal, Tripura and Odisha.

Method Of Cocking

1. Make the puffed rice crispy: Microwave for 30 seconds or dry roast in a pan over low or medium-low heat for 2â€“3 minutes, or until crunchy.
2. Prepare the vegetables: Boil, peel, and cut potatoes into 1 cm cubes. Finely chop onions, tomatoes, green chilies, ginger, and cucumber.
3. Prepare the spice mix: In a large mixing bowl, combine the following spice powders: red chili powder, cumin powder, chaat masala, garam masala, aamchur, and salt.
4. Combine the ingredients: Add the vegetables, coriander leaves, and peanuts to the spice mix.
5. Add other ingredients: Squeeze in some lemon juice, add green chutney, and drizzle with raw mustard oil and green-mango pickle oil.
6. Toss and serve: Add the puffed rice and toss everything together. Top with sev and serve.
