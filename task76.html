RASIYA MUTHIYA

Muthiyas are a Gujarati snack made from a variety of grains and besan, and are high in fiber and protein. They can be steamed or pan-fried, and cooked on medium heat to develop a crispy, flavorful texture.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. Gram flour: Also known as besan.
2. Wheat flour: Also known as atta.
3. Semolina: Also known as sooji or rava.
4. Fenugreek leaves: Also known as methi.
5. Grated bottle gourd: Also known as dudhi or lauki.
6. Spices: Such as chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin-coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala, carom seeds, fennel seeds, and asafoetida.
7. Other ingredients: Such as oil, sugar, salt, yogurt, ginger paste, garlic paste, and lemon juice.

Method Of Cocking

1. Combine the ingredients in a deep bowl and knead into a soft dough.
2. Divide the mixture into equal portions and shape each portion roughly into an oval by rolling it between your palms and fingers.
3. Grease the steamer plate with oil.
4. Place the muthiya in the steamer plate.
5. Cover the steamer pot and let the muthiya steam for 20-25 minutes.
6. Check if the muthiya is cooked through by inserting a knife or skewer inside to see if it comes out clean.
7. Cool and slice the muthiya into thick roundels.
8. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a pan.
9. Add 1 teaspoon mustard seeds, 1 teaspoon white sesame seeds, 1 sprig of curry leaves, and 1 pinch asafoetida.
10. Stir and let the mustard seeds crackle.
