RAGI DOSA

Ragi dosa, also known as nachni dosa, kelvaragu dosai, or finger millet dosa, is a South Indian crepe made from fermented ragi batter. Ragi, also known as red millet, is a nutritious ancient grain that originated in South Africa and is high in protein, calcium, iron, and dietary fiber.

Method Of Cocking

1. Wash and soak the ragi, urad dal, and methi seeds in water for 2 hours. Blend the ingredients into a thick batter, adding minimal water. Transfer the batter to a bowl and let it ferment for 6–8 hours. The batter should rise slightly and have a pouring consistency similar to buttermilk.
2. Heat coconut oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. When they sputter, add curry leaves, chopped green chili, and ginger. Sauté until fragrant, then add sliced onions and sauté until translucent.
3. Heat a tawa over medium flame. Stir the batter before making each dosa. Ladle a spoonful of batter onto the tawa and make a thin dosa. Drizzle oil around the sides and cook until golden brown. Flip the dosa and cook for a few more seconds, then remove and serve.
