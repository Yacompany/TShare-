Method Of Cocking

1. Heat 2 tbsp mustard oil in another pan or kadhai. Make sure to use a vessel with a large bottom so that the curry does not spill while cooking.
2. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 8 to 10 fenugreek seeds and a pinch of asafoetida to it.
3. Add ⅓ cup chopped onion. Stir and sauté on low to medium-low heat for 3 minutes.
4. Then add 1 tbsp chopped ginger, ¾ to 1 tbsp chopped garlic and 2 chopped green chillies. Stir and fry for a minute.
5. Now add 8 to 10 curry leaves, 2 dried red chillies (halved or broken).
6. Fry on low flame while stirring for a minute.
7. Then add the curd solution.
8. Shake well.
9. Reduce the flame to medium and bring the kadhi to a boil. Keep stirring it continuously so that the bottom does not turn brown. The kadhi will take about 14 to 16 minutes to cook.
10. When it starts boiling, reduce the heat and let it cook for another 6 to 7 minutes. Keep stirring it in between.
11. The kadhi is ready as shown in the picture below.
12. Now add onion pakoras to the curry and stir gently.
13. Cover with a lid and let the onion pakodas soak in it for 8 to 10 minutes.
14. Finally sprinkle some garam masala powder on the Punjabi kadhi.
15. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve Punjabi Kadhi Pakora hot with steamed rice or jeera rice, drizzled with a few spoons of ghee.
