TANDOORI CHICKEN

Tandoori chicken is a dish of chicken that's marinated in yogurt and spices and then roasted in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven. The dish originated in Punjab before India's partition and became popular in the late 1940s at the Moti Mahal restaurant in New Delhi.

Method Of Cocking

1. In a bowl, whisk together 3/4 cup of thick yogurt, 1 tablespoon of garlic paste, 1/2 tablespoon of ginger paste, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 2 tablespoons of Kashmiri chili powder, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, and 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil.
2. Cut deep slashes into the chicken pieces, about 3–4 places on each leg or thigh, or 2–3 places on each drumstick.
3. Brush the marinade all over the chicken.
4. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 5–6 hours, or overnight, turning occasionally.
5. Preheat the oven to 230°C/450°F/gas mark 8.
6. Line a roasting tin with foil and place a wire rack on top.
7. Put the chicken on the wire rack and cook for 10–15 minutes, or until it's cooked through and has charred marks.
8. The chicken is done when the juices run clear or you can pierce it with a knife.
