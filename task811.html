BABRU

Babru is a traditional breakfast recipe that originates from the Northern state of Himachal Pradesh. This lip-smacking dish draws a parallel to the commonly known poori, except that this one is stuffed.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. wheat flour.
2. split black gram.
3. salt as required.
4. refined oil.
5. baking powder.
6. water as required.

Item Quantity

1. 3 cup.
2. 1/2 cup.
3. 2 cup.
4. 1/2.

Equipment Name Used In Dish

Method Of Cocking

1. To prepare this delicacy first, soak black gram dal overnight or for 5-6 hours in water. Once done, drain the excess water and add the dal in a blender to grind it into a paste.
2. Now, in a bowl mix wheat flour with 1/2 cup of refined oil, baking powder and salt . Combine all the ingredients well and using water knead a soft dough. Seal the dough airtight, use muslin cloth if required. Leave it undisturbed for about half an hour.
3. When the dough is prepared, take small portions of it and roll it flat using a rolling pin (similar to preparing a chapati). Now in the centre of each portion add the black gram paste. Seal all sides of the potions to make sure the stuffing does not come out. Again roll it back to a circular shape similar to a poori.
4. Now, in a wok heat oil over medium flame. Gently slip the prepared babru in the oil and fry on both sides until they appear fluffy and evenly cooked. Transfer into a serving dish and serve hot with any vegetable, tamarind chutney or pickle.
