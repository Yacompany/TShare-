PANEER BHURJI

Paneer Bhurji is a popular North Indian dish. It is made from Indian cheese, herbs, and ground spices. Bhurji means fried, so it involves breaking cheese into pieces and cooking it with onions, tomatoes, and spices.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. 1–2 cups of chopped onions.
2. 1–2 medium tomatoes.
3. 1–2 green chilies.
4. 1 inch of crushed ginger.
5. 4–5 crushed garlic cloves.
6. 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and 15 ml of ghee.
7. 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds.
8. 1/4 teaspoon of fennel seeds.
9. 1/4 teaspoon of hing asafoetida.
10. 1 teaspoon of coriander powder.
11. 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric powder.
12. 1/4 teaspoon of red chili powder.

Method Of Cocking

1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
2. When the cumin seeds sizzle, add chopped onions and green chili.
3. Sauté until the onions are golden brown.
4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell goes away.
5. Add chopped tomato, salt, and turmeric.
6. Stir in red chili powder, garam masala, and kasuri methi.
7. Sauté until the raw smell goes away and the masala leaves the sides of the pan.
8. Add crumbled paneer and mix gently.
9. Cover and simmer for 3 minutes until the paneer is cooked.
10. Turn off the heat and add coriander leaves and kasoori methi.
