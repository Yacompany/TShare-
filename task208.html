SATTU

Sattu can be made by roasting and grinding horse gram or chana, maize, barley, or a mix of all these grains. For example, when chana is roasted and ground without being roasted, it becomes besan, but once roasted and powdered, it becomes sattu.

Method Of Cocking

1. Mix one tablespoon of sattu with lemon juice in a glass of water, stir, and drink. You can also add a pinch of salt, cumin powder, chaat masala, and lemon juice to a glass of water with two tablespoons of sattu, whisk to form a paste, fill the glass with water, and stir.
2. Knead plain dough for a parantha, then mix half a cup of sattu with coriander, cumin, ajwain, ghee, green chili, onion, lemon juice, salt, ginger and garlic paste, and mustard oil to form a thick filling.
3. Mix 1/4 cup of sattu with salt and lemon juice in a bowl, add a glass of water and mix well, then add chopped onion, cucumber, green chili, black pepper powder, roasted cumin powder, black salt, chaat masala, and coriander leaves, stir, and serve.
