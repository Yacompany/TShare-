MISSI ROTI

Missi roti is a flavorful, nutty flatbread that's a North Indian specialty, especially in Punjabi and Rajasthani cuisine. It's made from a mix of whole wheat flour, gram flour, and spices, and can be eaten for breakfast or lunch.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. 1 cup of wheat flour (atta).
2. 1/2 cup of chickpea flour (besan).
3. 1/2 teaspoon of salt (namak).
4. 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder (haldi).
5. 1 teaspoon of carom seeds (ajwain).
6. 1/4 teaspoon of cumin seeds (jeera).
7. 1/4 teaspoon of red chili powder (laal mirch).
8. 2–3 finely chopped green chilies (hari mirch).
9. 1/3 cup of finely chopped onion (piyaaz).
10. 5–6 sprigs of cleaned and chopped fresh coriander (cilantro) (hara dhania).
11. 1/2 inch piece of chopped or grated ginger (adrak).

Method Of Cocking

1. Mix the ingredients in a bowl or pan.
2. Add water and knead until the dough is smooth and soft.
3. Roll the dough into a 5 to 6 inch circle.
4. Place the roti on a hot tawa or skillet.
5. Flip the roti when the base is partially cooked.
6. Spread ghee or oil on the partly cooked part.
7. Flip again when the second side is cooked and brown spots appear.
8. Press the edges with a spatula so that the sides are also cooked.
9. Flip once or twice more so that the roti is evenly cooked.
