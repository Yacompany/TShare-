KASHMIRI MUJI GAAD

This is a unique Kashmiri style fish curry that is cooked along with some diced radish. It is very popular among the Kashmiri Hindu pandits. The gravy is spicy, tangy and with a mix of flavours coming mainly from the fennel and ginger powder and cooked in mustard oil for an authentic touch.

Method Of Cocking

1. Marinate the fish pieces with a pinch of salt, turmeric powder and red chilli powder for 10 minutes.
2. Heat oil in a pan and fry the fish pieces and the radish separately. Drain and keep aside.
3. Temper the same oil with shahjeera, cloves and cardamoms. Add all the dry spices mixed with some water. Saute till the oil separates.
4. Now add 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Gently drop in the fried fish, fried radish and green chilies. Cover and simmer till the gravy thickens.
5. Add the tamarind paste mixed with 1/2 cup water. Simmer further for 2-3 minutes and switch off the flame.
6. Serve with hot steamed rice for a sumptuous meal.
