DAL PITHI

If we talk about traditional dishes, then the name of Dal Peeth will definitely come up in Bihar. This dish has been specially prepared by generations. It is also called Dal Ki Dulhan. This dish is also prepared and eaten in some other places.

Method Of Cocking

1. Wash tur dal and moong dal properly and put them in the cooker. Take four times the amount of water as dal. That means add four cups of water to one cup of dal or as per your requirement. Add salt, turmeric and put the lid on the cooker and close it. After 2-3 whistles, turn off the gas and let the cooker cool down. Now let's prepare pithi or dulhan.
2. Take 1 cup wheat flour and gram flour in a plate. Add salt, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder and oil for kneading and mix. Add some water and knead soft dough. Now cover it and leave it for 10-15 minutes.
3. Now take some dough and roll it, make small balls of it, apply some dry flour and roll it into puri. Wet all the four edges of the puri and then stick two ends in the middle. Then stick the other two ends and make it in the shape of a flower. You can make flowers with three or four petals. Similarly, make the dough from the entire dough. Keep in mind that stick the edges of the dough together properly so that it does not open while cooking.
4. When the cooker cools down, open the lid and mix the dal well. Turn on the gas and keep the flame on medium to low. Now add all the prepared powder or dulhan to the dal. Cook on medium to low flame without closing the lid of the cooker. Check one powder. If it is cooked, turn off the gas.
5. Now add ghee in a pan to prepare the Tadka. Then add garlic, cumin seeds. When the garlic turns red, add curry leaves. When the Tadka is ready, take out the dal paste in a serving bowl and pour the Tadka on top and serve it hot. Potato Chokha and stuffed red chilli pickle taste very good with it.
