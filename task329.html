DAL BATI CHURMA

Dal Baati Churma is a traditional dish of Rajasthan, which is popular all over India due to its delicious taste. It is a very good combination of sweet and sour taste. Most people have the notion that making Dal Baati Churma is a very difficult task, but through my recipe you will know that making Dal Baati Churma at home is very easy.

Method Of Cocking

1. To make Baati, take 300 grams of coarse wheat flour in a vessel, add 1/4 cup semolina, 1 teaspoon carom seeds, 1 teaspoon salt and 1/2 cup ghee and mix the entire mixture well. Add water to the mixture and prepare a dough that is neither too tight nor too loose. Keep the dough covered for 20 minutes.
2. After 20 minutes, knead the prepared dough lightly. Shape it into a bati as per your liking. Preheat the oven on low flame for 5 minutes. Now place the prepared batis in it and put the lid on and shake well by turning them on medium flame. After the bati is ready, take it out of the oven and keep it in Desi Ghee.
3. To make Panchratna Dal, wash all the five dals thoroughly and put them in a pressure cooker and add 2 glasses of water. Now add salt as per taste and 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder and close the pressure cooker and keep it for 20 minutes for the dal to swell. After 20 minutes, cook the dal on the gas till 2 whistles. Keep the dal aside to cool.
4. To fry the lentils, add 4 teaspoons of Desi Ghee in a pan and heat it well. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 2 pinches of asafoetida, finely chopped 3 green chillies, finely chopped 7 garlic cloves and grated 2 inches of ginger. Now put all the things in hot ghee. Cook it well till it turns red. Now add finely chopped 1 large onion and fry it till it turns brown. Now add finely chopped 3 large tomatoes and cook it well.
5. To this mixture, add 1/4 teaspoon fennel powder, 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder, and 1/2 teaspoon garam masala and fry it well by adding some water until the tomatoes melt.
6. Add the boiled lentils to the prepared fried mixture and cook well. To temper the lentils, heat 3 teaspoons of Desi Ghee in a tempering pan and add 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds, 3 dried red chilies and 1/2 teaspoon Kashmiri chili and turn off the gas immediately. Now pour this tempering over the lentils.
7. To make churma, break 5 prepared batis and grind them coarsely in a mixer. Take it out in a bowl, add 1/4 bowl of brown sugar and 1/2 bowl of coconut powder to it. Chop almonds finely and add them and raisins to it. Now add 1/2 bowl of lukewarm ghee to it. Now mix it well. Take out the prepared churma in a bowl and garnish it with cashew nuts and almonds. Shape the remaining mixture into laddus with the help of hands and garnish with almonds on top.
8. I first took out the churma in a bowl and from the same churma mixture, I gave it the shape of a laddu. Thus, both my churma and churma laddu were ready.
9. Now serve Daal Baati, Churma and Churma Laddu in a serving plate (serving it with any pickle will enhance its taste even more). I have prepared the entire Daal Baati Churma with pure Desi Ghee, which doubles its taste. Rajasthan's most famous dish Daal Baati Churma is ready.
