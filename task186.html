BAJARA ROTI

Bajra roti, also known as bajra bhakri, is a gluten-free flatbread made from pearl millet flour, or bajra in Hindi. It's a staple food in many parts of India, and is often served with curries, sabzi, or kadhi for lunch or dinner.

Method Of Cocking

1. Heat water, but not boiling, and turn off the heat.
2. Add oil and salt to the water and mix well.
3. Add bajra flour to the water, or vice versa.
4. Mix with a spoon if the water is too hot, or with your fingers if it's warm.
5. Knead the dough until it's soft and smooth. If the dough is dry and crumbly, add some more water and knead.
6. Break off a piece of dough and press it with your hands to make it soft.
7. Make the dough round and expand it with your hands.
8. Flatten the dough to a 4–5 inch diameter.
9. Place the roti on a hot tawa.
10. Cook until small bubbles appear, then flip it immediately.
11. Gently press and cook the roti on both sides.
