SOTO (INDONESIA)

Soto is a traditional Indonesian soup category that includes meat or vegetable broth, shredded meat, vermicelli, and bean sprouts. It's usually served with rice or rice cakes. Soto ayam, or chicken soto, is a popular Indonesian dish lontong, noodles, and rice vermicelli.

Method Of Cocking

1. Skin the chicken and wash and remove excess fat.
2. In a large pot, combine stock, kaffir lime leaf, lemongrass, and salt, and bring to a simmer. Add the chicken and simmer until it reaches an internal temperature of 170°F, about 15–20 minutes. Remove the chicken from the broth, blot dry, and set aside.
3. Rinse bean sprouts with boiling water and drain. Cook noodles in salted boiling water. Slice shallots, celery, and peppers.
4. Put the chicken meat in the boiling bouillon. Divide the broth among bowls, and top with chicken, shallots, hard-boiled eggs, bean sprouts, and coriander leaves. Let each guest assemble their soup to taste.
