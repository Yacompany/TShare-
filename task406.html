KOBARI ANNAM

Kobbari Annam in Telugu, Tenginakayi Anna in Kannada, and Thengai Sadam in Tamil) is oh-so-easy to make, and tastes heavenly. I make this nutty, vegan, gluten-free rice dish often in the festival season as it is a preferred naivedyam and as a means of using some of the coconut I get as prasadam.

Method Of Cocking

1. In a heavy-bottomed wok/kadai, heat 1 tbsp coconut oil.
2. Add 1 tsp mustard seeds and wait till they splutter.
3. Now, add 1 tbsp udad dal and 1 tbsp chana dal, and stir-fry till light brown.
4. Next, add 6 to 8 split cashews and 1-2 tbsp peanuts.
5. Fry till the cashew turns light brown and the peanuts just start to pop.
6. Add 3 to 4 split dried red chillies and a few curry leaves, and stir-fry for a minute.
7. Turn the heat to low.
8. Add 1 cup freshly grated coconut and about 1 tsp salt and mix well.
9. Increase the flame to medium and stir fry the coconut till it just starts to change colour. Ensure that the coconut does not turn brown.
10. Turn off the heat.
11. Immediately, add the cooked and cooled rice, and mix well.
