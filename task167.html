KAJU KATLI

Kaju katli is a traditional Indian dessert that's famous for its rich history and culture, and its melt-in-your-mouth texture. The name comes from the Hindi words kaju (cashews) and katli (thin slices), and the sweet is often diamond-shaped and wrapped in edible silver foil.

Item Quantity

1. 2 cups (250g) whole raw cashew nuts.
2. 2 tablespoons (15g) whole-milk powder.
3. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder.
4. 10 tablespoons plus 1 1/2 teaspoons (125g) granulated sugar.
5. 1/4 cup (60ml) water.
6. Rounded 1/2 teaspoon ghee.
7. Edible silver leaf or vark, optional.

Equipment Name Used In Dish

Method Of Cocking

1. In a non-stick pan, combine Â¾ cup of sugar and 90 ml of water.
2. Cook over medium heat until the sugar melts and boils rapidly.
3. Add cashew powder and mix well until smooth and without lumps.
4. Add 1 teaspoon of rosewater and 1 teaspoon of ghee.
5. Continue cooking until the mixture thickens and starts to separate from the sides of the pan.
6. Remove from heat and let cool until it's easy to handle.
7. Roll the mixture out on a greased surface to a thickness of 1/4 cm.
8. Cover with silver leaf and let cool.
9. Cut into diamond shapes.
