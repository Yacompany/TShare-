JONNA ROTI

Jonna rotte ~ jowar ki roti is prepared in many Indian states and is not regional only to Telangana. It is a common preparation in rural areas. The first time I saw these rottelu made at home was few years ago when amma found out she was diabetic.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. Jonna Pindi ~ Jowar ka Atta ~ Sorghum Flour (more if required).
2. Water.
3. Salt (adjust to taste).

Item Quantity

1. ¾ cup.
2. ½ cup.
3. ¼ - ½ tsp.

Equipment Name Used In Dish

Method Of Cocking

1. In a saucepan bring water to boil and turn off the stove. Add salt. When the water is still hot, gradually add ½ cup + 1 tbsp. of flour, mix until flour is evenly mixed and comes together . Cover the pan and let it sit for 5 - 10 minutes until the dough is manageable with hand.
2. Transfer the dough to a plate or to a clean countertop and knead to make a soft dough. If necessary, add some flour. Dough should be softer than chapati or roti dough but not very soft and sticky. Keep the dough covered at all times.
3. Heat a griddle or tawa or a pan to roast rottelu.
4. Divide the dough into 3 equal parts.
5. Take one part of the dough, shape it into a ball, dust some flour and roll the dough ball into a circle. Edges will crack, even it with fingers. Do not apply to much pressure when rolling and gently roll the rotte, turning it around.
6. Gently lift the rotte and transfer to the hot griddle.
7. With a pastry brush or a kitchen towel, brush water on the top of the rotte. This is essential to keep the rotte from cracking.
8. Flip the rotte and cook on the other side until rotte is cooked on the other side.
9. Transfer to a plate and keep it covered or wrap it in a cloth or a kitchen towel.
