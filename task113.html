GOZPACHO (SPAIN)

Gazpacho is a cold, spicy soup or drink that originated in the southern regions of Spain and Portugal. It's typically made from raw, blended vegetables, such as tomatoes, cucumbers, red onion, and garlic, along with water, olive oil, and vinegar.

Method Of Cocking

1. Marinate the vegetables with the garlic, vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper for at least 3 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld together.
2. If using a blender, blend at high speed for at least 2 minutes, pausing occasionally to scrape down the sides.
3. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while the motor is running until the mixture is smooth and emulsified, like a salad dressing. If it's still watery, add more olive oil until it has a creamy texture.
4. To serve, top the soup with basil, croutons, and a drizzle of oil.
5. In a large bowl, combine chopped cucumber, peppers, tomatoes, garlic, spring onions, bread, salt, and pepper.
6. Add sherry vinegar and olive oil, and mix by hand.
7. Blend the vegetable mixture until smooth.
8. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
9. Serve ice cold with gazpacho ice cubes, chopped basil, olive oil, and toasts.
