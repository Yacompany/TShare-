RAGI MUDDE

Ragi muddas are gluten-free balls made from millet flour. Ragi is also called finger millet or mandua in South India and madde means soft ball in Kannada. Ragi Mudde is a popular food in rural areas of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. Wash and soak 1/2 cup of rice for 15 minutes.
2. In a pressure cooker, add the rice, 2 cups of water, and salt to taste.
3. Pressure cook for 3 whistles, then remove the lid and check if the rice is mushy.
4. Add 1/2 cup more water if needed, then add 1 cup of ragi flour and cover.
5. Simmer for 5 minutes, then mix the flour and rice with a rolling pin.

Method Of Cocking

1. Put water in a heavy bottom pan and boil it.
2. Add salt and oil to it and boil it.
3. Add rice and cook on low flame for a few minutes.
4. Now put ragi flour in water and do not stir.
5. Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low flame for 5 to 6 minutes.
6. Mix the mixture well, so that no dry flour is left.
7. Keep the stove on low flame and keep mixing for a few minutes.
8. The mixture will form a ball and move away from the sides of the pan without sticking.
9. Put one spoon ghee in a plate and spread it, so that the balls do not stick.
10. Put the mixture in a plate and make a ball with a spatula.
