SOUPE I'OIGNON GRATINEE (FRANCE)

Soupe à l'oignon gratinée, or French onion soup, is a classic French dish of onions cooked in stock or water and served with croutons or bread and cheese on top.The soup is often associated with Paris in the winter and is a staple on menus in Parisian bistros and brasseries.

Method Of Cocking

1. Sauté onions in butter until translucent and softened.
2. Sprinkle with flour and stir.
3. Add broth, wine, and cognac.
4. Simmer until flavors meld, about 15 minutes.
5. Season to taste.
6. Pour soup into oven-safe bowls.
7. Top with toasted bread slices.
8. Sprinkle with a generous amount of strong cheese.
9. Broil until cheese is melted and bubbly, about 5 minutes.
