LAUKI CHANA CURRY

Lauki chana dal is a Punjabi-style North Indian curry made with bottle gourd (calabash), chickpea lentils, tomatoes, chilies, and spices. It's tangy, a bit spicy, and flavorful, and can be served with roti or paratha.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. 1.5–2 cups of chopped bottle gourd (lauki).
2. ¼ cup of chana dal (split and husked Bengal gram or split chickpea).
3. ½ tsp of mustard seeds.
4. ½ tsp of cumin seeds.
5. ½ cup of chopped tomatoes.
6. 1 tsp of chili powder.
7. ¼ tsp of turmeric powder.
8. 1 tsp of coriander-cumin seeds powder.

Method Of Cocking

1. Soak chana dal in water for 30 minutes.
2. Peel and cut lauki into medium or small pieces.
3. Heat oil in a wide kadai or pressure cooker.
4. Add whole spices like cumin, cloves, cinnamon, caramel, and bay leaf.
5. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
6. Add ginger-garlic paste or chopped garlic and sauté until aromatic.
7. Add chopped tomatoes and sauté until soft.
8. Add spice powders like salt, Kashmiri red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and turmeric powder.
9. Add chana dal and lauki.
10. Cover and pressure cook for 4–5 whistles. If there's too much liquid, simmer without the lid to dry it out.
