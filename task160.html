MOTI CHOOR LADOO

Moti choor ladoo is a popular Indian dessert made from gram flour or besan (chickpea flour), sugar, water, and sometimes nuts and seeds. The flour is made into tiny balls called boondi, which are fried in ghee or oil and then simmered in sugar syrup until they plump up.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. This recipe makes ladoo and calls for 2 cups of besan.
2. 1 1/2 cups of water.
3. 1/4 teaspoon of saffron food color.
4. 1 cup of sugar.
5. 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder.
6. 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice.
7. 2 cups of virgin olive oil.
8. 2 pinches of baking soda.

Method Of Cocking

1. Heat sugar and water in a saucepan until the sugar dissolves, stirring often.
2. Once it reaches one string consistency, remove from heat and add cardamom powder, rose essence, a few drops of orange food coloring, and lemon juice.
3. Transfer the cooked boondis to the pan with the sugar syrup and add melon seeds.
4. Mix well with a ladle.
5. Cover and let sit for at least 10 minutes so the syrup is absorbed.
6. Open, fluff, mix, and shape the ladoos.
7. Arrange on a plate, cool, and store.
