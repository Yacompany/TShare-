NAAN KHATI

Naan khatai, also spelled naan khattay or naan katai, are shortbread biscuits that originated in the Gujarat region of India during the Mughal era. The name comes from the Persian words naan, meaning "bread", and khatai, meaning "biscuit".

Method Of Cocking

1. Mix all dry ingredients together in a bowl.
2. In a separate bowl, whisk together ghee, powdered sugar, and cardamom powder.
3. Add the flour mixture to the ghee mixture and fold well.
4. Add milk and knead into a soft dough.
5. Refrigerate the dough for 20 minutes.
6. Roll the dough into balls and place them on a lightly greased cookie sheet.
7. Press pistachios into the tops of the balls.
8. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 15–17 minutes, or until light golden and crisp.
9. Remove from the oven and let cool completely.
10. Store in an airtight container.
