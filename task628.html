PATRANI MACHHI

Generally, Ghevar is very famous in Rajasthan. Ghevar, which is found in every house during the festival of Sawan and Rakhi, is liked by everyone. Now you do not need to wait for Sawan or Rakhi for this. Yes, you can make it at home in an hour.

Method Of Cocking

1. First make one string syrup.
2. Take solid ghee in a large bowl and add ice cubes one at a time. Keep stirring the ghee rapidly, if needed, keep adding more ice cubes. Keep stirring till the ghee turns white.
3. Now make a thin mixture of milk, flour and water. Dissolve yellow food colour in some water. The mixture should be thin (while making Ghevar, the mixture should come out easily from the spoon).
4. Now take a steel or aluminum vessel, whose length should be at least 12 inches and five to six inches thick. Now fill half the vessel with ghee and heat it.
5. When the ghee starts to smoke, fill the mixture in a 50 ml glass and pour it in the centre of the vessel in a thin stream.
6. Now let the mixture set properly and then pour another glass of the mixture into the vessel by swirling it around the edges.
7. The Ghevar will leave the sides of the vessel and small holes will appear in the centre, then take it out carefully and place it on a wire sieve.
8. Keep the syrup in an open vessel. Dip it in hot syrup, take it out and put it on a wire to squeeze out excess syrup.
9. When it cools down, cover the top of the Ghevar with silver foil. Sprinkle a little saffron on it, add chopped dry fruits and a pinch of cardamom powder and serve.
10. It can also be served with readymade rabdi.
