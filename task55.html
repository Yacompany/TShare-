MAKKE KI ROTI SAAG

Makki di roti and sarson ka saag is a culinary pairing that often includes homemade butter or ghee. Sarson ka saag is a vegetarian dish from northern India that's rich in nutrients and antioxidants, and helps with digestion. Makki di roti is high in fiber and can help you feel full.

Method Of Cocking

1. Wash and roughly cut the saag, like sarson and palak. You can also pressure cook the saag, then mash it until it's coarsely ground, add makki atta, and stir.
2. In a cooker, heat oil, then add jeera, onion, ginger, garlic, and green chili. Fry until the onion changes color.
3. Add the saag to the cooker and cook on low heat for 15â€“20 minutes.
4. Just before serving, heat ghee and add hing and red chili to it. Mix it with the saag.
5. Take makki flour, add warm water to it, and knead into a smooth dough. Roll out the roti on a grease or baking paper. Heat a griddle or tava, and start cooking the roti. Flip it once cooked from one side. Cook the roti on the gas flame directly, or on the tava if you do not have gas cooktop. Once cooked, brush with ghee, and serve warm.
