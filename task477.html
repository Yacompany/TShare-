BANANA FRITTERS

Growing up in a Jamaican household has its perks. Every Saturday morning there was Beres Hammond or Bob Marley playing in the house as we cleaned and if we children were good, mom made us these delicious fritters.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. bananas.
2. vanilla extract.
3. cinnamon.
4. nutmeg.
5. brown sugar.
6. Pinch of salt.
7. purpose flour.
8. Oil for frying.

Item Quantity

1. 3 ripe.
2. 1 teaspoon.
3. ½ teaspoon.
4. ¼ teaspoon.
5. 3 Tablespoon.
6. 1 cup.

Equipment Name Used In Dish

Method Of Cocking

1. First, grab three ripe bananas and smash them together with a fork until they are mashed and a little watery.
2. Then add your vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, brown sugar, and salt to bananas. Next, add your all purpose flour. I use a sifter to add my flour. If you don’t have one, no worries, you can still make the recipe. I just find the sifter allows my flour mixture to incorporate into my mashed bananas easier.
3. Stir with a spoon until your mixture is fully combined. Your batter should not be too thick or thin but should flow easily off a spoon.
4. Pour about ¼ cup oil in a 10 inch skillet and heat it over medium-high heat. Once your oil is hot, pour the fritters in pan by the spoonful.
5. Once bubbles appear in the top of the fritter and the bottom is golden brown, flip the fritters and cook until the other side is brown as well. Mine take about 2 minutes per side to brown.
6. Remove fritters from skillet and drain on paper towel. Ya mon.
