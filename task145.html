CHEESE STUFF PARATHA

Cheese paratha is an Indian flatbread made with whole wheat flour dough and a generous amount of grated cheese. The dough is kneaded until smooth, then rolled out, folded like a dumpling, stuffed with cheese, and rolled again.

Method Of Cocking

1. In a bowl, combine flour, salt, and oil, then gradually add water and knead until the dough is soft and smooth. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
2. Grate 1 1/2 cups of cheese and add chopped coriander, ginger, garlic, onion, or other finely chopped vegetables for flavor and crunch. Season with chili powder, chaat masala, garam masala, and a pinch of salt.
3. Pinch off two small balls of dough, flatten them, and dust with flour. Roll each ball into a 4–5 inch round. Place the cheese filling in the center of one round, leaving about 1 inch of space at the sides. Gently place the second round on top and press and seal the edges. Lightly dust the paratha with flour and roll it into a 7–8 inch round.
4. Heat a tawa over high heat and add ghee, spreading it evenly. Place the paratha on the tawa, cook for a few seconds, flip it over, and spread with oil. Cook until golden brown on both sides, then remove from heat and serve hot.
