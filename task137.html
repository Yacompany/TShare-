BHARWA ALOO KACHORI

Aloo kachori, or potato kachori, is a deep-fried flatbread stuffed with a spicy potato filling. It's a popular breakfast snack in Northern India, especially in Mathura-Vrindavan in Uttar Pradesh, and is often served with aloo sabzi, curd, chutneys, or pumpkin sabzi.

Method Of Cocking

1. Sift flour and semolina, then add salt, baking powder, and oil. Mix well by hand, then knead with lukewarm water. Cover and let dough rest for 20–30 minutes.
2. Boil potatoes and peel, then break into small pieces. Heat oil in a wok, then add cumin seeds and wait for them to crackle. Add coriander powder, green chili, ginger, and salt.
3. Pinch off a small ball of dough, flatten it with a rolling pin, and place about a tablespoon of filling in the center. Gently press the dough to flatten and round it, then add a few drops of oil if needed.
4. Heat oil for deep frying in a deep pot over low to medium heat. Carefully lower the kachori into the hot oil and fry until cooked. Use a slotted spoon to drain excess oil.
