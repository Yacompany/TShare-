SUJI HALWA

Suji halwa, also known as sheera, is a traditional Indian dessert or pudding made from semolina, ghee, sugar, and other ingredients. It can be served for breakfast or dessert and is often offered as prasadam in temples or on special occasions.

Method Of Cocking

1. Heat ghee in a thick-bottomed pan over low or medium-low heat..
2. Add sooji (semolina) and roast, stirring constantly, until it turns golden brown and smells nutty, about 5–10 minutes.
3. In a separate pan, bring water and sugar to a boil to make a sugar syrup.
4. Microwave a mixture of water, sugar, and cardamom in a bowl for two minutes, stirring once, to dissolve the sugar.
5. Slowly add the sugar water mixture to the sooji, mixing well.
6. Reduce heat to low and let the halwa cook until the ghee separates and the suji is glossy and runny, about 5–7 minutes.
7. Add yellow food coloring to taste, mix well, and garnish with almonds and dry fruits.
8. Serve hot with puris and channay.
