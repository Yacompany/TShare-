NAAN KHATAI

Nankhatai, also spelled Naan Khatai, Naan Khattay, or Naan Katai, are shortbread cookies that originated in Gujarat, India. They are popular in Northern India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and Myanmar, and are often found in Indian bakeries.

Method Of Cocking

1. Beat ghee and caster sugar together in a bowl until pale, light, and fluffy.
2. In a separate bowl, combine all the dry ingredients.
3. Mix the wet and dry ingredients together to form a dough.
4. Refrigerate the dough for 20 minutes.
5. Roll the dough into balls and place them on a lightly greased baking sheet.
6. Press chopped pistachios into the tops of the balls.
7. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for about 15–17 minutes, or until golden and crispy..
