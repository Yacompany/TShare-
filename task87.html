BADANEKAI TUMBAGAALI

Badanekayi, also known as brinjal or eggplant, is a warm season crop that requires a long growing season with moderate temperatures and plenty of sunshine. It can be grown in plains throughout the year, but the rabi season is best.

2. Stuff the brinjals with prepared masala paste.
3. Heat oil in a large kadai and add mustard seeds and curry leaves.
4. Add the stuffed brinjals and sauté until they change color.
5. Add the remaining masala paste and ½ cup of water, and mix well.
6. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes or pressure cook for 2 whistles.
7. Remove from heat when the oil separates and the ennegayi is cooked.
8. Serve with joloda rotti or chapati.

Method Of Cocking

1. Cut the brinjals in an X-shape without removing the stalk.
2. Stuff the brinjals with prepared masala paste.
3. Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a large kadai and sputter 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and a few curry leaves.
4. Add the stuffed brinjals and sauté on medium heat until they change color.
5. Add the prepared masala paste and ½ cup of water, mix well, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes or pressure cook for 2 whistles.
6. Enjoy with joloda rotti or chapati.
