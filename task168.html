SWEET BOONDI

Sweet boondi, also known as meethi boondi, is a classic Indian dessert of deep-fried chickpea flour balls soaked in cardamom-scented sugar syrup. Sweet boondi can also be made savory by adding nuts, curry leaves, chili powder, and salt.

INGREDIENT'S

Items No

1. Gram flour (besan).
2. Baking soda.
3. Oil.
4. Sugar.
5. Water.
6. Crushed cardamom.
7. Yellow food coloring.

Item Quantity

1. 1 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
2. 1 1/2 cup water.
3. 1 cup sugar.
4. 2 cup ghee.
5. 2 pinches saffron.
6. 1/2 teaspoon green cardamom.
7. 8 almonds.

Equipment Name Used In Dish

Method Of Cocking

1. Heat ghee in a pan over medium heat.
2. Pour batter onto a perforated spoon and tap the edge to release small droplets into the ghee.
3. Deep fry the boondis until they are crisp but not brown.
4. Drain the excess oil from the boondis on a paper towel.
5. In a pan, mix sugar and water well so it is melted.
6. Bring it to a boil and cook till it attain a single string stage.
7. Take it off the heat and add in cardamom powder, clove powder, food coloring and mix well.
8. Add boondis to this syrup and mix well.
9. Garnish with chopped almonds and cardamom powder.
10. Allow the boondis to cool completely.
11. Loosen the boondis with your finger until each droplet separates.
