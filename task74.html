PURI KI LAPSI

Lapsi of Puri is a traditional Guyanese dessert, consisting of lapsi (a white, thick pudding) sandwiched between a puri (small fried flatbread). This sweet is usually made in the Hindu community and offered to Hindu deities, especially Durga, Kali, and Ganga. However, it can also be eaten as a sweet.

Method Of Cocking

1. First of all dry roast the porridge.
2. Take out the porridge in a bowl, add 3 spoons of ghee and keep it covered.
3. Add water to the jaggery and let it dissolve.
4. Keep milk boiling in a pan.
5. Add 1/2 bowl of water in it.
6. Let it boil well.
7. Cut the dates into small pieces and add them.
8. Grind cloves, cardamom, cinnamon powder and nutmeg and add.
9. Add ghee porridge.
10. When cooked a little, add dissolved jaggery and cook.
11. Add raisins and chopped nuts.
12. Add dry coconut.
